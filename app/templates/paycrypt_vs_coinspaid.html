PayCrypt vs CoinsPaid

Both PayCrypt and CoinsPaid help you accept crypto payments. The right choice depends on whether you run a betting / casino operation or a broader merchant business.

  • PayCrypt is designed around high-frequency deposits for betting platforms and casinos.
  • CoinsPaid is a large crypto payments provider serving many verticals (exchanges, merchants, and more).
  • This page focuses on fit: when a specialised betting gateway is useful vs when a general provider is enough.

If you run a betting platform or casino

The main question is not "Which brand is bigger?" but "Which setup gives my players fast, predictable deposits with clean reporting?".

  • You likely settle mostly in USDT on TRC20 or similar low-fee networks.
  • You care about reconciling every ticket and dispute with on‑chain evidence.
  • You may have multiple brands/skins under the same license.

PayCrypt is intentionally narrow: fast deposits, webhooks into your own platform, and admin tools that mirror how betting operators actually work.

Using PayCrypt alongside a larger PSP

Many operators combine a specialist gateway with a larger payments provider:

  • Use PayCrypt for high‑volume USDT deposits and betting‑specific flows.
  • Use other processors for card payments or general merchant businesses.
  • Keep settlement and risk decisions in your own back‑office instead of delegating everything.
